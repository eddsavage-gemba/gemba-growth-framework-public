# Growth and Craft

- Invests actively in building foundational skills and core craft competencies.
- Seeks out coaching, feedback, and help from more experienced colleagues.
- Begins to share early learnings with peers (e.g., small demos, pairing).
- Demonstrates curiosity and a willingness to learn from mistakes.
- Shows visible enthusiasm for learning and improving; asks thoughtful questions.
- Tackles unfamiliar tasks with curiosity rather than hesitation.
- Manages workload responsibly and asks for support when capacity or wellbeing is at risk.

# Personal Effectiveness

- Works well within team structures, following established processes and norms.
- Communicates progress and blockers clearly and early.
- Receives feedback constructively and acts on it quickly.
- Demonstrates professionalism and reliability.
- Recruitment: No involvement expected.

# Client Value and Impact

- Gets to know the client's mission and starts learning the domain.
- Understands how their work fits into the bigger picture.
- Delivers work with support and asks questions when things aren't clear.

# Delivery Quality

- Completes clearly defined tasks with support.
- Applies core skills (analysis, testing, documentation, research) correctly.
- Uses team tools and processes as intended.
- Produces work that meets expectations when guided.

# Community and Contribution

- Engages positively in Gemba culture: communities, socials, knowledge-sharing.
- Contributes ideas, energy, and enthusiasm to internal initiatives.
- Volunteers for simple tasks that improve team or community experience.
